Explore the University of Kent Campus

Click below to explore an interactive 3D model of the University of Kent Canterbury campus. Scroll down to find out more information about the project.

Explore the map

Welcome the University of Kent 3D Campus map! The whole of the Canterbury campus has been recreated as a fully interactive 3D model that you can view in your web browser. Just make sure you are running a modern browser such as the current version of Google Chrome, Firefox or Safari for Mac. Unfortunately Internet Explorer 10 or below does not support the technology used on this website and therefore the site will be unable to run on those browsers.


Below you will find an explanation of some of the main features of the campus map which will help you get the most out of the experience. Remember to click on the 'Explore' button above to start the application.

Building Information

Clicking on a building on the campus map will reveal a short description of the building and in some cases further information on rooms or accommodation facts.

Single rooms in a building can be selected from a list within the information pop-up which will take you to the Kent timetabling website for further information about that room.

Each building has a category such as: Teaching, Community or Admin. This helps to quickly differentiate between them without having to read the description.

Transport Routes

Information about public transport can be toggled onto the map to help when you need to get around the campus.

The overlays include a visualisation of the three main bus routes and the various cycle and footpaths that connect the campus, as well as which car parks are available to visitors and where to lock up your bike on campus.

Clicking on the purple bus icons on the map will show you a quick overview of the bus times for that particular bus stop.

Data Overlays

Data overlays are 3D bar graphs which display data about a selection of buildings at once allowing you to compare them.

The Complete University Guide has been used to provide data about the performance of individual subjects across the University of Kent. This includes student satisfaction and UCAS entry points required to enter the course.

Accommodation data is also included to give an idea of the different types of study bedrooms available to Undergraduate and Postgraduate students.

Search Function

To quickly and easily find a building or room you a looking for simply start typing the name into the search bar at the top of the page and select one of the suggested results.

You will then be taken to taken to the corresponding 3D building where you can find any general information or specific rooms that you need.

This feature will make exploring the UKC campus much easier if you need to find a specific building quickly.

Social Feed

The Social Feed allows you to kep up to date on the latest information from the University of Kent as well as get a taste of student life on the campus. The UKC maintains a large number of different Twitter accounts which cover a broad range of areas to help interact with students on a daily basis. The Social Feed brings them all together for the first time along with Instagram pictures tagged with #KentUni.

Filter the feed

Clicking the filter icon will reveal a list of filters that will change which combination of streams are visible. By default all streams will be shown, but by selecting a combination of the streams individually you can display only those that you wish to view at one time.


Combine filters to get the latest information from the University of Kent that is most important to you.

Sort the results

The sort icon allows you to choose how to display the various tweets and instagram photos in the feed. The default option is set to show the items in a grid which fits to the size of your screen, however you can choose to switch to a list view which will display the items one after another.


You can also sort the items by the default 'newest to oldest' or select 'oldest to newest' instead.

Kent Facts

When viewing the campus map you may notice small question marks which you can click to reveal various facts about the university. These usually include information on new investments and developments that have been carried out the previous year. For more facts like these you can visit the Enhancing your experience page of the Kent website.

Mobile

This project has been designed to work on all devices that support WebGL technology. This means that if you have the latest smartphone or tablet you can explore the campus just like you can on a desktop or laptop computer. This website is designed to fit to the screen size of your device and work with touch controls as well as a traditional mouse and keyboard input.

Desktop

For the most immersive experience view the project on your desktop or laptop using Google Chrome.

Tablet

If you have a new Android tablet running Google Chrome then go ahead and give it a try!

Mobile

Android phones such as the Nexus 5 should be able to run the project perfectly on the go.
